French [FRE courses - includes FCS, FSL]

The following timetable information is the most current information available since the publication of the 2011-2012 Registration Handbook.

Students may only register in the course(s) for which they have the stated prerequisites.

For language (FSL) courses, first-time students must register in the course recommended by the Departments Placement Test ( www.lang.utoronto.ca/placement/french ). The test must be taken prior to registration and before the beginning of classes in any given term. Please allow three to five working days to obtain the test results.

Note that, since 100, 200, 300 and 400-level FSL courses correspond to levels of competence in French, students may be recommended to take a course at a level higher than their year of study. Students who do not have access to a computer capable of managing sound files should write to french.placement@utoronto.ca to make an appointment to take the test at the Multimedia Centre.

For FRE240H1: students must register in each of the following for a total of 4 hours per week: 1 two-hour lecture (L5101) and one two-hour tutorial (T0101, T0201, T5101 or T5102).

For FRE241H1: students must register in each of the following for a total of 3 hours per week: 1 two-hour lecture (L5101) and one tutorial (T0101, T0201).

For FRE272H1: students must register in each of the following for a total of 3 hours per week: 1 two-hour lecture (L0101) and one tutorial (T0101, T0201, T0301 or T0401).

For E indicator courses: Applicants must complete the Ballot for Admission and the Description of Project Form, available at http://www.french.utoronto.ca/courses/145 and return them, duly signed by their supervisor, to Linda Lamisong, room 226, Odette Hall. An electronic version of the application must be forwarded to french.undergraduate@utoronto.ca. The deadlines are as follows: September 9, 2011 for F, H and Y courses; January 6, 2012 for S courses; April 6, 2012 for the 2012 summer session.
