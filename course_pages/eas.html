East Asian Studies [EAS courses]

The following timetable information is the most current information available since the publication of the 2011-2012 Registration Handbook.

For assistance students may call 416-946-3625 for general information or see the Program Administrator of the Department of East Asian Studies, Robarts Library, Room 14087, 130 St. George Street, Monday to Friday, 10:00 a.m. to 12:00 p.m. and 2:00 to 4:00 p.m.

FOR ALL FIRST YEAR LANGUAGE COURSES the instructor reserves the right to remove students from the class who are found to have inappropriate prior knowledge of the language.

FOR ALL SECOND TO FOURTH YEAR COURSES the instructor will remove students who do not have the proper prerequisites.

CHINESE LANGUAGE COURSES: All students who wish to enrol in any modern Chinese language course must undergo a placement interview. Students will be asked to answer questions about their background and their previous experience in language learning. Students will be interviewed on a first-come-first-served basis. Interviews for a course will stop once the course is full. You must bring two placement interview forms to your interview. Go to http://eir.library.utoronto.ca/easic/login.cfm (username: Chinese; password: 2011/2012) and download these forms. Fill them out and bring them to your interview.

CHINESE LANGUAGE COURSES INTERVIEW SCHEDULE: For EAS100Y1, EAS101Y1, 200Y1, EAS201Y1, EAS300Y1 and EAS400Y1: Students must have their interview in one of the following two sessions: Friday, April 8, 2011, 1:00 to 4:30 p.m. or Tuesday, September 6, 2011, 1:00 to 4:30 p.m., Robarts Library, Room 14213. Students who need to be further assessed will take the Chinese language placement tests on Friday, September 9, 2011 at 1:00 p.m.
CHINESE LANGUAGE WRITTEN PLACEMENT TESTS: Chinese language written placement tests take place twice a year. In the academic year 2011-2012, placement tests will be held on Friday, September 9, 2011 at 1:00 p.m. and Friday, March 2, 2012 at 1:00 p.m. Students who wish to take the Chinese language written placements must hand in their registration forms at least two weeks in advance. Please go to http://eir.library.utoronto.ca/easic/login.cfm (username: Chinese; password: 2011/2012) and download these forms.

KOREAN LANGUAGE COURSES: Enrollment priorities go to students who meet the prerequisite requirements. Those students who satisfy the prerequisite may enroll in Korean language courses through SWS by themselves. All other students who wish to enroll in EAS110Y1 or any other Korean language course for the first time are subject to placement interviews. Based on the interview, some students may be asked to take a placement test on a designated date. After a student passes an interview, the Department of East Asian Administrator will attempt to register him/her via ROSI only once.

JAPANESE LANGUAGE COURSES: All newly admitted students (which does not apply to those who repeat the first year) MUST attend the interview held in RL 14147 from 9:00 a.m. to 12:00 p.m.on Tuesday, August 30, 2011. The interview date will not be rescheduled for any reason. (Interview form is available at SWS during the first round of enrolment (this applies only to courses with the enrolment indicator PE). If you fail to do so, please go to the EASIC website at http://link.library.utoronto.ca/easic/login.cfm (username: Japanese, password: 20112012).
Date of the test: Wednesday, August 31, 2011 Time: 10:00 to 10:30 a.m. (Check-in), 11:00 a.m. to 1:00 p.m. (Test)
Location: TBA (It will be posted at the EAS website), and send to, celia.sevilla@utoronto.ca. Requests should include the course number, section and student name and number. For any other inquiries, please contact the instructors directly by e-mail (address are available on the EAS website).

For EAS103H1: if enrolment is high, T0301 F12 and T0402 F1 will be added.

For EAS105H1: if enrolment is high T0102 F10, T0202 F11, T0302 F12, T0402 F1 will be added.
